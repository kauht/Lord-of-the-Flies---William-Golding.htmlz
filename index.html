Note from the artist
Dear reader,
Thank you for picking up this graphic novel edition of
Lord of the Flies
. There’s a lot
to say about the impact of William Golding’s classic, and many scholars and academics
have done so. But to be honest with you, I’m not a historian or a literary expert of
any kind. As a lifelong fan, I can only tell you that
Lord of the Flies
has meant a lot
to me personally. It took my breath away right at the first read, during my English
class at school. Perhaps as a teenager, hanging between a carefree childhood and the
independence of adulthood, something in the novel mirrored what I was experiencing
within myself.
To be asked to create the first
Lord of the Flies
graphic novel adaptation was an
incredible honour, as well as the scariest thing I’ve ever committed to. But I do
believe it’s a story that needs to be told again and again. I’ve met people who fled
life-threatening conditions, war and captivity, even in my own family. One question
returns to me every so often: when do the rules of our carefully crafted civilization end,
and was it really there in the first place? There’s no book in the world, I think, that
investigates these questions as masterfully as
Lord of the Flies
.
In creating this adaptation, I did not want to simply create an illustrated version of the
novel, but rather to let the graphic novel format do what it does best: adding meaning
through composition, colour and atmosphere. If you are reading the English-language
edition, you will notice that each phrase is taken from the original work. Using these
texts allowed me to treat the work with respect, keeping Golding’s beautifully composed
sentences intact. Yet there was still room for me to tell the story in the way I chose to.
In the next few pages, you will see some of my early sketches and the various stages of
the drawing process.
Thank you, again, for picking up this graphic novel. I do hope it will make
Lord of the
Flies
accessible to many new readers. I’m convinced this story will remain relevant for
as long as humanity exists, which confirms its genius, but makes for a sad truth at the
same time.
Aimée de Jongh, January 2024
